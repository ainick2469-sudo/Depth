# Frontier Depths Game Vision

## One-Sentence Identity

Frontier Depths is a western-fantasy dungeon ascension RPG where players descend through an endless living dungeon from a growing frontier outpost, build class-specific power, hunt bounties, defeat milestone bosses, and eventually unlock an Ascension Route where climbing upward scales much harder than going down.

## Core Fantasy

The player is a frontier adventurer pushing into a supernatural dungeon below a rough outpost. The tone should feel like dust, iron, lantern light, strange magic, old ruins, desperate contracts, and class-driven mastery.

## The Big Hook

Going down is progression. Going up is escalation. The dungeon lets the player build power by descending, then challenges that power by asking them to climb back through harsher, stranger versions of what they survived.

## Descent Phase

The descent is the regular run loop: enter from town, clear rooms, take rewards, pursue bounties, improve class power, and decide when to return or push deeper.

## Ascension Phase

Ascension is a future high-pressure route back upward. It should scale harder than the descent, remix threats, and make every floor feel like the dungeon is resisting escape.

## Frontier Town Role

The town is the anchor. It is where the player returns, upgrades, accepts bounties, unlocks services, tracks reputation, and sees long-term progression become visible.

## Living Dungeon Role

The dungeon is the threat. It should feel reactive, layered, and hostile rather than like a generic sequence of rooms. Room purpose, enemy packs, secrets, rewards, and future bosses should reinforce that identity.

## Class Identity Role

Classes must feel different through resources, skill trees, combat loops, and upgrade choices. Gunslinger currently uses Health, Stamina, Focus, chamber reload timing, pistol whip, and weapon handling. Mana is reserved for future classes.

## Long-Term 50,000-Floor Philosophy

The enormous floor count is a progression fantasy and systems goal, not a demand for 50,000 hand-authored floors. The game should use bands, themes, enemy families, milestones, bosses, town unlocks, and ascension rules to make the depth feel meaningful.

## What This Game Must NOT Become

- Do not turn Frontier Depths into generic medieval fantasy sludge.
- Do not add more enemy names before existing enemies play differently.
- Do not let imported asset packs replace the project architecture blindly.
- Do not make every class use the same resource loop.
- Do not make basic ammo scarcity the core Gunslinger fantasy unless it returns later as special ammo.
- Do not treat the town as a menu skin; it is the long-term anchor.
